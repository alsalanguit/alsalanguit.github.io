Hello everyone!

Thank you for visiting my page and for viewing my work. My name is Andrea Salanguit, I study at Wake Tech Community College and specialize in Cybersecurity.

I grew up in a small town called Nasugbu in the Philippines with my parents and two sisters. At the age of 15, my family immigrated to Italy which began a challenging period in my life in many ways: from having to leave my friends and family, adjusting to an unfamiliar country, learning a new language, and adapting to a culture that was very different from the one I knew. While in Italy, I completed my secondary education and then spent three years studying linguistics at the Universit√† per Stranieri in Siena, Tuscany. It was during this time that I met my now-husband who was visiting on a study abroad from the US. One thing led to another and now I find myself once again in a new adventure here in America.

In my first years in the US, I worked in the service industry. It was a breath of fresh air working in a fast-paced environment and interacting with people, especially after being so isolated during the pandemic. It was a fitting job for someone like me who was trying to plan their next step in life.

Serving is a physically and mentally taxing job so after 2 years of doing this, I find myself in need of change, so, upon discovering that Wake Tech offered Cybersecurity program, I decided it was best for me to pursue school.

I currently do not precisely know what type of work I will do but I am hoping to have an idea as I go through the courses of my degree and learn as much as I can.

About my photography:

I got my first starter DSLR camera when I was 15 years old. As someone who has always been interested in art, especially in photography, this gave me an opportunity to grow in this field. Eager to learn, I started watching tutorial videos and reading about photography and eventually teaching myself how to use editing programs such as Adobe Lightroom.

It brings me a deep level of satisfaction when I see my pictures come to life as I edit them, especially when emotions and memories are attached to them. I mostly do landscape photography but I also put myself out of my comfort zone by doing something different from time to time. In many of my pictures, you will see my favorite muse, my dog Mia.

Slowly, I am building my portfolio as a photographer. My goal is to one day be able to take pictures manually using analogue cameras and have a darkroom of my own.
